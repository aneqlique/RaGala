Try out some soothing soups from Palawan!

CREDITS TO : Lagum Adventure

Chao Long Noodles

 
This dish originated in Vietnam but found a warm welcome in Palawan thanks to Vietnamese refugees who settled on the island in the 1970s. Over time, Chao Long has become a beloved local delicacy. It features a rich, flavorful broth with a hint of sweetness (a Filipino twist!), thin rice noodles, and your choice of meat (usually beef or pork). It's a comforting and delicious dish that perfectly embodies the welcoming spirit of Palawan.

Sinigang sa Bayabas

 
This Filipino soup features everyone's favorite sour staple - pork belly - simmered with vegetables and tomatoes in a unique broth. But here's the twist: the tangy base comes not just from tamarind, but also from fresh guavas! This lesser-known variation offers a delightful sweet and savory experience. The guava adds a subtle sweetness that complements the savory pork and vegetables, while a touch of tamarind keeps the signature tang alive. It's a perfect dish for those who enjoy the classic sinigang but want to explore new flavor profiles.

Imbao

 
This local delicacy features the region's prized imbao, or mangrove clams, simmered in a savory broth. Simple yet bursting with flavor, the soup brims with the essence of the island.
