A P P E T I Z E R S

Try out some popular appetizers from Palawan!

CREDITS TO : Lagum Adventure

Tamilok

 
Unveiling a surprising Palawan delicacy: tamilok. Nicknamed "woodworm" but actually a saltwater clam, these mollusks burrow into mangrove wood. While not the easiest catch, locals brave slick riverbeds to harvest them. Eaten raw with vinegar and chili, tamilok is an adventurous treat for those seeking a unique taste of the island.

Lato

 
This bright green seaweed, also known as sea grapes, is a familiar sight throughout the Philippines, but it thrives in Palawan's crystal-clear waters. Not only is lato delicious in salads with a delightful popping texture, but it's also packed with vitamins and minerals, making it a healthy and flavorful island treat!

CREDITS TO : Greek Gecko

Kinilaw

 
This Filipino ceviche features fresh, local catches like tanigue (Spanish mackerel) or tuna. The magic lies in the tangy marinade - a vibrant mix of vinegar, citrus juices, and aromatics like ginger, onions, and chilies. Sometimes, a touch of creamy coconut milk is added for a unique Palawan twist. The result? A light, flavorful dish that perfectly captures the essence of the island's bounty.
