PALAWAN PROVINCE

The Philippines' Last Frontier

 
It is a dream destination for travelers seeking both relaxation and adventure. Palawan is an archipelago with over 1,700 islands. It boasts rich biodiversity, with a lot of its land and marine areas still relatively untouched. Whether you're seeking for healing trip on pristine beaches, adventure in untouched landscapes, or a glimpse into a rich cultural heritage, Palawan has something for everyone.

Island Hopping

Palawan isn't just a breathtaking archipelago adorned with limestone cliffs, hidden lagoons, and pristine beaches - it's a haven for the adventurous soul. Each island boasts its own distinct personality, culture, and, of course, a healing nature scenery waiting to be explored. Picture scaling dramatic cliffs and rewarding yourself with experiences through Island hopping with yourself, friends, special love ones, and family.

Matinloc Island

Matinloc Island is a small island located in El Nido, Palawan, Philippines. It's known for its pristine beaches, clear waters, and limestone cliffs. There is also a Matinloc Shrine, perched on a cliff overlooking the sea, that is dedicated to the Virgin Mary. It offers stunning views of the surrounding islands.

Pinabuyatan Island

El Nido boasts countless breathtaking islands, and Pinagbuyutan Island is a jewel in its crown. This secluded paradise offers a tranquil escape for those seeking pristine beauty. Unlike some of the busier islands, Pinagbuyutan offers a serene atmosphere, perfect for relaxation and soaking up the natural beauty.

Snake Island

Don't let the name fool you! Snake Island, located just off the coast of El Nido, Palawan, is a paradise for sunbathers and beach lovers. This islet gets its name from its long, winding sandbar that resembles a snake during low tide, connecting the mainland to the island.

BRIEF

History of Palawan

 
The island paradise of Palawan boasts a fascinating history that extends back millennia. Archaeological evidence suggests Palawan was a cradle of Philippine civilization, with indigenous groups like the Tagbanwa and Palaw'an establishing residence for over 22,000 years. Their unique alphabet and cave paintings offer a glimpse into their rich cultural heritage.

Fun activities to try

Beyond the picture-perfect beaches and turquoise waters, Palawan transforms into an adventure playground for thrill-seekers and explorers. Each island boasts its own unique set of activities, promising an unforgettable experience for every type of traveler.

Prepare to be mesmerized by a natural wonder unlike any other: the Puerto Princesa Underground River. Located on the western coast of Palawan, this UNESCO World Heritage Site boasts the distinction of being the longest navigable underground river in the world.


The Kayak activity offers a unique and unforgettable way to explore the island's hidden coves, secluded lagoons, and stunning coastlines and lakes. This activity is available in different spots in Palawan such as in Coron, El Nido, and Puerto Prinsesa.
